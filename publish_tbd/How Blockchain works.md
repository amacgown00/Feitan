# How a Blockcahin Works

A blockchain is an imutable ledger that stores information that is openly published but renders the sources or nature of its transactions untraceable through cryptographic technology. These methods are appealing to those who want to perform actions without needing to know or trust the people involved. A widely known method is sending cryptocurrency, but thanks to certain platforms, the possibilities are endless. Applications include escrow, stock purchases, supply chain. 

Imagine exchanging someone who was kidnapped for ransom. The danger is that there is no guarantee  the other side will hold up their end of the bargain or what will happen afterwards. There is no trust. With smart contracts, you can write code that will execute a series of actions that both sides know will happen, so the parties involved do not need to trust each other. 

## Creating New Blocks

On a blockchain platform, a set of transactions will occur within a specific amount of time. When that time is up, they are collected and validated by a node running blockchain software. Once the validation is complete, the block is created and added onto the end of the chain, which can be viewed at any time. There are people who have dedicated computers running this software 24/7 that constantly validate and add new blocks.
